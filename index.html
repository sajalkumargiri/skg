Theoretical Light-Matter Chemistry Group

We leverage machine learning (ML) and quantum dynamics (QD) methods to investigate excited state dynamics of molecules and exciton systems in plasmonic nanocavities. Strong plasmon-exciton coupling leads to the formation of hybrid plasmon-exciton states, commonly referred to as polaritons or plexciton. Our research explores such polariton/plexciton states and their impact on photocatalysis, energy/charge transfer, and spectroscopy.

Research

Machine Learning Light-Matter

Understanding of light induced excited state dynamics in molecules and nanomaterials is often challenging due to their inherent complexities. Are all these complexities essential for capturing the effective dynamics that we are interested in? If a subspace exists that encodes the essential dynamics with minimal complexity, it would significantly simplify the problem. To address this, we integrate quantum dynamics with machine learning methods and explore excited state dynamics of nanoparticle-molecule complexes.

Learn more

Cavity QED Chemistry

We investigate the dynamics of energy/charge transfer in molecules under strong plasmon-molecule coupling inside nanocavities. Noble metal nanomaterials exhibit efficient light absorption across a wide spectral range, primarily due to localized surface plasmon resonance (LSPR). Nanocavities confine plasmon modes, that can strongly couple with the molecular electronic states, resulting in the formation of polaritonic states similar to molecular polaritons within Fabry-Pérot cavities. Our research develops theoretical frameworks for modeling polariton assisted photocatalysis, energy/charge transfer rates, and spectroscopy using cavity quantum electrodynamics (QED).

Learn more

Nonlinear Spectroscopy with Quantum Light

We study nonlinear spectroscopy of organic molecules under a full quantum description, treating both molecules and light quantum mechanically. One of the main objective of this research is to obercome the fundamental limit of convential light spectroscopy incorporating the quantum nature of light. Entangled photons have shown promise in enhancing two-photon absorption and improving photochemical reaction efficiencies. To this end, we develop theoretical frameworks to address fundamental understanding like entanglement transfer in light-matter interactions and their connection with the nonlinear spectroscopy.
